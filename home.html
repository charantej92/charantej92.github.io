    Research Interests

    Signal Processing and Machine Learning

    Summary of key results

    In Signal Processing, my research primarily focuses on identifying support structures for which the Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) can be computed fast and designing corresponding algorithms. My most recent work explores the connection between additive structure of the frequency support and computational complexity of the DFT.

    In Machine Learning, my research mainly centers around Adversarial Machine Learning, with a particular emphasis on examining these issues from a perceptual standpoint. My recent work investigates the relationship between the imperceptibility and semantic significance of adversarial perturbations.
